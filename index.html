Australian Music Festivals 2023 Snapshot

Australia has a vibrant and growing live music industry, and the ultimate form of live music events are festivals. Music festivals generally feature dozens of artists and can last multiple days. Further, they can feature many different genres of music. This visualization presents a snapshot of the current Australian festival scene, with all data referencing festivals held in 2023.

These days, large festivals often host artists that represent a wide range of genres. In this page, these are represented as "Multiple Genres". Generally, this includes pop, hip-hop, indie, and EDM.

The festival capital of Australia is New South Wales, which hosted 151 festivals in 2023. This was followed by Victoria and Western Australia. However, these are by far the most populated states. Comparing to normalised festivals per million people, the Northern Territory hosts by far the most festivals compared to its population, holding 13 festivals despite having a population of just 253,000.

This heatmap shows the distribution of festivals by state and genre. As expected, multiple genre festivals dominate across the board. However, Victoria has a higher proportion of Jazz-focused festivals, reflecting Melbourne's cultural reputation. Additionally, the Northern Territory has a higher proportion of Indigenous festivals, indicative of its significant Indigenous population.
